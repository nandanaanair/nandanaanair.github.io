UX Case Study · Streaming

Netflix Redesign

Figma Team: Nandana Nair & Sristi Shetty India
Open prototype ↗

The Problem

Users are frustrated by seeing already watched content repeatedly in their recommendations — making it hard to discover something new.

"It would be great if we could mark something as seen."
34/39
Users frustrated by watched content in recommendations
97%
Wanted a separate section for already-watched content

Design Process

01

Empathize

Conducted a comprehensive survey to dive into users' minds. The message was clear — users wanted a better way to manage their viewed content.

  • 34 out of 39 users felt frustrated when previously watched content appeared in recommendations
  • 97% of respondents wanted a dedicated separate section for content they'd already watched
  • Competitor analysis — Disney+, Amazon Prime Video, and YouTube — revealed no platform handled this elegantly
  • Key insight: users wanted to rediscover old favourites on their own terms, not have them pushed at them
02

Define

Combining user feedback and competitive insights, we hypothesised that:

Providing a separate section for watched content or greying out viewed items in recommendations could significantly improve user satisfaction.
03

Ideate

Visualised the user's journey through storyboarding, highlighting moments of frustration and opportunities for enhancement. Two potential solutions emerged:

  • Give a separate section for the watched content
  • Grey out the watched items in recommendations
04

Prototype

Low-Fidelity Wireframes — Sketched out basic user flows, mapping how these changes would integrate seamlessly into the Netflix app.

High-Fidelity Wireframes — Brought the concepts to life with three signature design additions:

  • Mark as Seen icon (✓) — a quick-tap checkmark on any title to flag it as watched
  • Already Watched icon (⏱) — a subtle clock badge visible on watched content in rows
  • "Already Watched" section — a dedicated shelf tucked inside Profiles & More, letting users browse and revisit their viewing history without polluting their home screen

Key Takeaways

Netflix's user-centric design is a testament to its success — but also a rewarding opportunity to explore. This project deepened my understanding of user-centred design and the iterative nature of the design process. Identifying areas for improvement within such a polished platform was a challenge, but also incredibly rewarding.

This project ignited my passion for crafting exceptional user experiences, and I'm eager to apply these learnings to future endeavours.

UX Design · Music Therapy

Spotify MoodGuru

Figma Solo project 20 days India
Open prototype ↗

The Idea

You're scrolling through Spotify, searching for the perfect soundtrack to match your mood. Countless playlists later, you're still feeling unsatisfied.

What if your music could adapt to your emotions, creating a personalized soundtrack for every moment?

That's where MoodGuru comes in — your personal DJ on Spotify. Imagine a world where your music adapts to your emotions, creating a personalized playlist for every moment.

Idea Genesis

INTRO

Introduction to music and Spotify — discovering how deeply personal music listening is

SELF

Personal experience — searching endlessly for music that fit my exact mood, never quite satisfied with generic playlists

INSPR

Inspiration from similar apps — observing how other services attempted (but fell short of) mood-based recommendations

AHA

Realization of Spotify's potential — seeing the opportunity to build something deeper within an existing ecosystem

Design Process

01

Empathize

Conducted user surveys on Spotify listening habits and emotional relationship with music. Key findings:

  • Most users are generally happy with Spotify, but playlists rarely match their current mood or activity
  • Users want to input their mood via text or voice — not just pick from preset categories
  • Strong demand for saving personalized mood playlists for future re-use

Competitive Analysis — vs Calm, Headspace & Breethe

  • Spotify already leads in personalized music and tailored recommendations
  • Competitors in the wellness space excel at mood-tracking but have limited music variety
  • Gap identified: no app combined Spotify's library depth with truly adaptive mood-based curation
02

Define

Defined four core problem areas:

  • Lack of personalisation — recommendations feel generic, not emotionally aware
  • Generic playlists — curated lists don't adapt to the user's evolving mood
  • Limited music therapy options — no structured path for relaxation or focus
  • Lack of customisation — users can't fine-tune recommendations beyond basic genres
Integrating personalized music therapy suggestions will lead to increased user excitement and satisfaction — matching tunes to their mood is bound to elevate their experience.
03

Ideate

Storyboard — Followed Max, a developer on a tight deadline. He opens Spotify for focus music, scrolls through playlist after playlist, can't find the right vibe, gets frustrated and distracted. MoodGuru solves this in one tap.

Translated ideas into lo-fi wireframes: the MoodGuru button on the home screen, a mood search bar, and a voice-input animation screen leading to personalized playlist results.

04

Prototype

Hi-fi prototype featuring the complete MoodGuru flow:

  • MoodGuru entry point on the Spotify home screen
  • Text search bar — open-ended mood input ("I need to focus", "feeling low energy")
  • Voice search with animated listening state — accessible for hands-free use
  • Results screen — curated mood-matched playlists from Spotify's existing library
05

Test

Ran usability testing sessions on the hi-fi prototype. Users found the voice input especially intuitive and appreciated that MoodGuru didn't replace their existing library — it added a new emotional layer on top of it.

What's Next

The potential for personalized music experiences is vast. Here are some areas of improvement to explore:

  • Refining mood recognition beyond text and voice input
  • Allowing users to create custom mood labels
  • Tracking mood changes over time

Key Takeaways

Crafting the MoodGuru was a thrilling journey into the intersection of music and emotion. While the initial results are promising, I'm eager to continue exploring this space and building experiences that feel deeply personal.

UI Design · Dashboard · Visual Design

Freelancer Dashboard

Figma Solo project India
Open prototype ↗

Objective

To create a clean, modern, and user-friendly interface where freelancers could easily navigate their tasks, finances, and notifications — all in one place, with no friction between what they need to know and what they need to do.

Key Features

TASK

Task Management

  • Overview of current and upcoming tasks, each with a due date and brief description
  • Color-coded tags to differentiate between task types — e.g. priority tasks in red
  • The "+" icon lets users add new tasks instantly, staying on top of their responsibilities
PROJ

Project Status

  • Helps freelancers keep track of ongoing and pending projects
  • Each project card shows percentage of completion and deadlines at a glance
  • Thumbnail image + "view details" button for quick navigation to project actions
FIN

Finance Chart

  • Visually represents the freelancer's earnings over the past months
  • The shaded area in the chart provides a comparative view, showing fluctuations in income
  • Helps freelancers assess financial performance and plan ahead

Design Process

01

Ideate

Started by listing out all the ideas, then cut short to the most important features a freelancer actually needs daily — avoiding feature bloat.

02

Wireframe

Created rough paper wireframes focusing on task and finance management — iterated twice before arriving at a layout that balanced information density with clarity.

  • Initial iteration: lo-fi paper wireframe
  • Final iteration: refined lo-fi paper wireframe before going digital
03

High-Fidelity Design

Moved to Figma to create the high-fidelity design, choosing a pastel color palette for a professional yet friendly feel. The color-coded tags make it easy to parse information at a glance without cognitive overload.

Key Takeaways

This project reinforced that dashboard design is fundamentally about reducing cognitive load. A freelancer opens this screen when they're switching contexts — between client work, admin, and finances. Every element had to earn its place. The constraint of making information dense but not overwhelming was the most valuable design lesson here.

UI/UX Design · App Redesign · 2023

Domino's Pizza Redesign

Figma Solo project Jost font

The Problem

Domino's is the largest pizza company in America, operating across 90+ countries and 17,100+ stores. Despite its scale, the mobile app felt cluttered and confusing — finding what you wanted required unnecessary scrolling through a disorganised menu.

"I kept getting lost in the menu. I just wanted to order a pizza, not go on a treasure hunt."

Personal experience with the app's confusing navigation became the catalyst for this redesign — the goal: create a minimal, user-friendly interface that makes ordering feel effortless.

Design Language

Retained Domino's brand identity (white, black, red) while introducing a fresh design layer — light blue and teal accents for the redesigned screens, using Jost as the primary typeface for clarity and approachability.

Key Screens Redesigned

SIGN

Sign In & Login Page

  • Added a convenient country code selector for international users
  • Easy one-tap toggle between Sign Up and Log In — no more navigating back
HOME

Home Page

  • Clearly visible delivery location button — users always know where they're ordering to
  • Search by product — find what you want in one click, no full menu scroll
  • Visually distinct, tappable item buttons for a more tactile experience
  • Quick access to Profile Settings from home and a dedicated Favourites section
MENU

Menu Pages

  • Replaced chaotic horizontal scroll with an organised, scannable menu layout
  • Added a Recommendation section for better discovery
  • Keyword-based search — tap a keyword to jump straight to those results
  • Selected choices shown as a separate, uncluttered section as you scroll down
CART

Cart Items Page

  • Highlighted total amount and item count — always visible at a glance
  • Improved upsell buttons designed to actually attract attention (not just sit there)
  • Quick Customize button for last-minute changes without leaving the cart
PROF

Profile Settings Page

  • Decluttered and reorganised into a clean, breathable layout
  • Settings are grouped logically — account, preferences, orders — no more hunting

Key Takeaways

Redesigning a well-known app comes with a unique challenge: respecting the brand while fixing what's broken. This project sharpened my ability to audit an existing experience critically — separating what users expect from what actually frustrates them. Every screen decision was driven by reducing friction in the most common ordering path.

UI Design · Interaction · Animation

Nike Parallax Effect

Figma Solo project Interactive prototype
Open prototype ↗

About the Project

A Nike landing page concept built around immersive parallax scrolling and motion design. The goal was to demonstrate how interaction design and animation can turn a static product page into a cinematic, brand-forward experience.

"Just Do It." — brought to life through movement, depth, and visual storytelling.

What's Inside

HERO

Hero Section — Parallax Entry

The Nike shoe floats against a deep, moody dark background with a red gradient sweep. As the user scrolls, layers move at different speeds — creating depth and pulling focus to the product.

GRID

Product Grid — "Help you tap into your inner beast"

Three product cards (Nike Instant 1, 2, 3) animate into view with staggered transitions. Each card has a View More link and a bold Shop Now CTA button.

NAV

Navigation & Interaction

Minimal top nav — Men | Women | Kids | Customize — with wishlist and cart icons. Clean, focused, and brand-consistent with the dark aesthetic.

Design Highlights

This prototype is an exploration in motion-first UI design — using Figma's animation and scrolling tools to simulate parallax depth. It was a study in how interactivity can elevate a product's perceived premium quality without changing the information architecture at all.

UI Design · Interactive · 3D Carousel

Desi Dawat

Figma Solo project Interactive prototype
Open prototype ↗

About the Project

देसी-dawat. is an Indian restaurant landing page concept built around an interactive 3D carousel that showcases the menu's signature dishes. The design celebrates Indian food culture through warm, earthy visuals — cream, sand, and terracotta tones — with rich photography at the centre.

A feast for the eyes before the food even arrives.

Design Concept

FEEL

Aesthetic & Tone

Warm beige/cream backgrounds evoking a traditional Indian dining ambiance. Typography is clean and bilingual — Hindi script logo alongside English content — grounding the brand culturally.

DISH

Today's Specials Carousel

Each carousel card features a signature dish — Tandoori Tango Delight, Creamy Cottage Curry, Fiery Spice Fusion, Spiced Chickpea Delight — with a large hero food photo, dish description, and ingredient preview thumbnails.

NAV

Navigation

Simple 4-item nav — Home | Specialities | About Us | Contact — keeping the focus on the food and making ordering/exploration effortless.

Design Highlights

This project was an exploration in 3D depth within a 2D interface — using Figma's prototyping tools to simulate a carousel with perspective, layering, and rotation. The challenge was making the interaction feel physically satisfying while keeping the food as the hero of every frame.
